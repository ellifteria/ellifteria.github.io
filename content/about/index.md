# Hi! I'm Elli

I'm an undergraduate student at [Northwestern University](https://www.northwestern.edu/) majoring in [Computer Science](https://www.mccormick.northwestern.edu/computer-science/) and [Mathematics](https://www.math.northwestern.edu). I'm interested in computational mathematics and using math to develop algorithms for solving problems and answering scientific questions. I'm also a big proponent of [free software](https://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.en.html), [technological](https://redecentralize.org) [decentralization](https://medium.com/daia/decentralized-ai-manifesto-e79de7ea78b2), and the [solarpunk movement](https://www.re-des.org/a-solarpunk-manifesto/).

## Research interests

I'm passionate about computational mathematics research. I'm very interested in how we computationally and mathematically represent physical and social systems and how we develop efficient algorithms for answering questions about and solving problems for those systems. I hope to approach this through the lens of mathematics. Besides being beautiful and worthy of studying for its own sake, I want to investigate how to apply unexplored mathematical properties and ideas to computational algorithms to build a smarter, safer, and more environmentally conscious future.

My current work is focused on evolutionary computation in artificial intelligence. As a member of the [Xenobot Lab](https://www.xenobot.group), I'm investigating evolutionary algorithms and open-endedness in artificial intelligence and artificial life. You can see some of my work on [my GitHub](https://www.github.com/zoex308)!

I previously worked in the [Leonard Lab](https://www.leonard.northwestern.edu) where I  developed an [open-source software package](https://www.github.com/zoex308/pyflowbat) for analyzing flow cytometry data, specifically for mammalian synthetic biology applications. I also did a bit of wet-lab work characterizing mammalian synthetic transcription factors.
